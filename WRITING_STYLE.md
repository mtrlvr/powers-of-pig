# Writing Style Guide

Based on The Economist style guide. Read this before writing documentation, user-facing copy, or markdown files.

## Core Philosophy

Clear thinking is the key to clear writing. Think what you want to say, then say it as simply as possible.

## Simplicity

Use short words, short sentences, and short paragraphs.

- Short sentences are more memorable and easier to read
- If a sentence feels long, split it
- One idea per paragraph

## Word Choice

Prefer everyday words over formal alternatives:

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| obtain | get |
| purchase | buy |
| commence | start |
| terminate | end |
| utilise | use |
| manufacture | make |
| relinquish | give up |
| subsequently | later |
| regarding | about |
| approximately | about |
| endeavour | try |
| numerous | many |
| sufficient | enough |

Ask yourself: would I use this word talking to a friend?

## What to Avoid

**Jargon** — Technical terms alienate readers unfamiliar with them. Explain or avoid.

**Clichés** — Worn phrases weaken impact. Find original, specific language.

**Filler words** — Cut "basically", "actually", "currently", "essentially" unless they add meaning.

**Euphemisms** — Be direct about difficult subjects.

## Tone

- Do not be stuffy — use the language of everyday speech
- Do not be arrogant — those who disagree are not necessarily stupid
- Do not be hectoring — persuade with reasoning, not assertion
- Do not boast — let the work speak for itself

When expressing opinions, use arguments and evidence. The aim is to persuade, not just assert.

## Structure

Write like an essay: beginning, middle, end.

- Each piece should be a coherent whole, not bits stitched together
- Each paragraph should be "a unit of thought, not of length" (Fowler)
- Paragraphs should follow logically — ideally suffering if even one sentence is cut

## Active Voice

Prefer active constructions:

| Passive | Active |
|---------|--------|
| The ball was hit by John | John hit the ball |
| Errors were made | We made errors |
| It was decided that | We decided |

Active voice is direct, clear, and shorter.

## Opening Lines

Catch attention from the start. Do not waste sentences setting scenes or sketching background. Hold the reader by how you unfold the tale.

## Editing

Read through several times. Edit ruthlessly. Cut anything superfluous.

Every word must earn its place. Unadorned, unfancy prose is usually all you need.
